A Glimpse into the Mahabharata

The Mahabharata is the longest epic poem in the world, attributed to the sage Vyasa. It narrates the story of the Kurukshetra War and the fates of the Kaurava and Pandava princes, who are cousins embroiled in a power struggle.

The epic explores complex themes of duty, justice, and morality, featuring famous characters like Arjuna, Bhishma, and Draupadi. The Bhagavad Gita, a sacred text of Hindu philosophy, is part of this epic and features a dialogue between Lord Krishna and Arjuna on the battlefield.

The Mahabharata teaches valuable lessons about the consequences of war, the importance of righteousness, and the complexities of human nature.
